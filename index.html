AMW 2018

12th Alberto Mendelzon International Workshop on Foundations of Data Management
Cali, Colombia
May 21 – 25, 2018

  • Register now!

Since 2006, the Alberto Mendelzon International Workshop on Foundation of Databases and the Web (AMW) brings together top researchers from all over the world, creating the opportunity to discuss and spread research results.

The co-located AMW Summer School provides an opportunity for Latin American students (graduate and undergraduate), researchers, and practitioners, to interact with the international data management academic community. AMW is a way to honour the memory of Alberto Mendelzon, by promoting research ties with Latin America.

Speakers

Meet our invited speakers

Miriam Fernandez

Knowledge Media Institute (KMi)

Miriam Fernandez is a Research Fellow at the Knowledge Media Institute, Open University, and a senior member of the Social Semantics and Web Science group.

Vanessa Murdock

Amazon

Vanessa Murdock is a Science Manager at Amazon. Her research focuses on understanding user focus and attention online.

Hung Q. Ngo

RelationalAI

Hung Q. Ngo was a professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo from 2001 to 2015. From 2015, he started working for a couple of startups.

Pablo Barceló

University of Chile

Pablo Barceló is an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Chile. He received his PhD from the University of Toronto

Fei Chiang

McMaster University

Fei Chiang is an Assistant Professor in Computer Science at McMaster University in Canada and Faculty Fellow at the IBM Centre for Advanced Studies.

Denis Parra

PUC Chile

Denis Parra is Assistant Professor at the Department of Computer Science, in the School of Engineering at PUC Chile. He holds a Ph.D. in Information Science

Martín Ugarte

Free University of Brussels

Martín Ugarte is a postdoctoral researcher at the Free University of Brussels (ULB) in Belgium, where he has been working for the last two years under the Laboratory for Web & Information Technology.
